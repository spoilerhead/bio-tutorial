CC   -!- FUNCTION: DNA polymerase III is a complex, multichain enzyme
CC       responsible for most of the replicative synthesis in bacteria
CC       (PubMed:2932432). This DNA polymerase also exhibits 3' to 5'
CC       exonuclease activity. The alpha chain is the DNA polymerase
CC       catalytic subunit (PubMed:2932432). It is tethered to replicating
CC       DNA by the beta sliding clamp (dnaN), which confers extremely high
CC       processivity to the catalytic subunit, copying a 5.4 kb genome in
CC       11 seconds, a speed of at least 500 nucleotides/second at 30
CC       degrees Celsius (PubMed:2413035). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2413035,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2932432}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Deoxynucleoside triphosphate + DNA(n) =
CC       diphosphate + DNA(n+1). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2932432}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: The DNA polymerase III holoenzyme complex contains at
CC       least 10 different subunits organized into 3 functionally
CC       essential subassemblies: the Pol III core, the beta sliding clamp
CC       processivity factor and the clamp-loading complex. The Pol III
CC       core (subunits alpha, epsilon and theta) contains the polymerase
CC       and the 3'-5' exonuclease proofreading activities
CC       (PubMed:2040637). The polymerase is tethered to the template via
CC       the dimeric beta sliding clamp processivity factor. The clamp
CC       loader (also called gamma complex) assembles the beta sliding
CC       clamp onto the primed template and plays a central role in the
CC       organization and communication at the replication fork. The clamp-
CC       loading complex contains delta, delta', psi and chi, and 3 copies
CC       of either or both of two different DnaX proteins, gamma and tau.
CC       The DNA replisome complex has a single clamp loader (3 tau and 1
CC       each of delta, delta', psi and chi subunits) which binds 3 Pol III
CC       cores (1 core on the leading strand and 2 on the lagging strand)
CC       each with a beta sliding clamp dimer. Additional proteins in the
CC       replisome are other copies of gamma, psi and chi, Ssb, DNA
CC       helicase and RNA primase (PubMed:20413500, PubMed:22157955).
CC       Interacts with the beta sliding-clamp subunit via the peptide Gln-
CC       Ala-Asp-Met-Phe (residues 920-924) (PubMed:11573000).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11573000, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2040637,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20413500, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22157955}.
